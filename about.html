About Me

Proffessional Details

I am currently a PHD scholar in the Department of Computer Science and Information Technology, Central University of Jammu, Jammu and Kashmir, 181143 - India. I have completed my master's degree in Computer Science and Applications with 7.70 CGPA in 2018.

I have cleared the National Eligibility Test (conducted by National Testing Agency under University Grants Commission, India) four times consecutively (June 2019, December 2019, June 2020, and DEC 2020 & JUNE 2021 cycle). Additionally, I have cleared the Himachal Pradesh State Eligibility Test (HP-SET) in 2019.

I have worked as a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Information Technology, University of Jammu, Jammu from August 2019 to February 2022. I have gained valuable experience in teaching and guiding my students in different research areas. Moreover, as a lecturer I have (i) organized one week National Level E-Quiz on “Big Data Systems” (NLQBDS-2k20) as Organizing Secretary at Department of Computer Science & IT, Kishtwar Campus, University of Jammu, Kishtwar on 30 May-05 June 2020 and (ii) organized a Webinar on “Lifelong Learning Skills” as organizing Secretary at Department of Computer Science & IT, Kishtwar Campus, University of Jammu, Kishtwar on Monday, 12th July 2021.

I have published 13 Research papers (5 SCIE, 2 ESCI, 3 Scopus and 3 UGC-Care Journals), 3 Book chapters and 4 Patents. I am working as an editor and academic editor in the Journal of Research in Diabetes & Metabolism and Journal of Biomed Research respectively. I am also working as a reviewer in IEEE Communications Magazine, Computers in Biology and Medicine, Scientific Reports, PeerJ, New Generation Computing etc.

I am dedicated to fostering an interdisciplinary research strategy, as well as enhancing the research community via collaboration with scholarly organizations and by providing resources to help researchers effectively communicate their findings. To support and tackle some of the world's most pressing problems, I think it is essential to work with other members of the research community, including writers, societies, libraries, and others.
